Viññāṇa

Have you ever paused to ask: “What *knows* a thought?”

We usually think of “consciousness” as a thing we have.

But in Buddhism, it’s not a thing — it’s a process. A happening.

Consciousness is like a candle’s flame — flickering, momentary, not a thing but a happening.

Viññāṇa is the Pali word often translated as “consciousness” — but it’s not what we might assume.

What Is Viññāṇa?

Maybe we don’t need to *define* consciousness right away.

Maybe we just need to listen.
